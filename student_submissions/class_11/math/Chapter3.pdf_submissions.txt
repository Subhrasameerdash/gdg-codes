1.  A pendulum swings through an angle of 30 degrees. If the length of the pendulum is 75 cm, find the total distance its tip travels after making 10 complete oscillations. Express your answer in terms of π.
Sample Answer:
Okay, here's a sample answer suitable for a CBSE Class 11-12 student based on the provided NCERT chapter content.  The answer is broken down into clear steps with explanations.

**Answer:**

**Understanding the Problem:**

We need to find the total distance the tip of a pendulum travels after 10 complete oscillations, given the angle of swing and the pendulum's length. Each swing forms an arc of a circle.  The length of the arc is the distance the tip travels in one swing.

**Steps:**

1.  **Convert the angle to radians:**
    The angle of swing is given as 30 degrees. We need to convert this to radians because the formula *l = rθ* (where *l* is arc length, *r* is radius, and *θ* is the angle in radians) requires the angle to be in radians.

    We know that 180 degrees = π radians. Therefore,

    30 degrees = (30/180) * π radians = π/6 radians

2.  **Find the arc length of one swing:**

    The length of the pendulum is the radius of the circular arc, so *r* = 75 cm.  The angle is *θ* = π/6 radians.

    Using the formula *l = rθ*, the arc length of one swing is:

    *l* = 75 * (π/6) = (75π)/6 = (25π)/2 cm

3.  **Consider one complete oscillation:**

    One *complete oscillation* means the pendulum swings to one side *and* back to its starting position.  Therefore, in one complete oscillation, the tip travels *twice* the arc length we calculated in step 2.

    Distance of one complete oscillation = 2 * (25π)/2  = 25π cm

4.  **Calculate the total distance for 10 oscillations:**

    Since the pendulum makes 10 complete oscillations, the total distance traveled is:

    Total distance = 10 * (25π) = 250π cm

**Final Answer:**

The total distance the tip of the pendulum travels after making 10 complete oscillations is **250π cm**.

2.  Given that sin(θ) = p, where π/2 < θ < π, express all other trigonometric ratios in terms of *p*. Justify the sign of each ratio you determine.
Sample Answer:
Okay, here's a sample answer to the assignment question, tailored for a CBSE Class 11-12 student, based on the provided NCERT chapter summary and solved examples.

**Answer:**

Given: sin(θ) = *p*, where π/2 < θ < π. This means θ lies in the second quadrant.  We need to find all other trigonometric ratios in terms of *p* and justify their signs.

1. **Cosine (cos θ):**

   We know that sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1.  Therefore, cos²(θ) = 1 - sin²(θ) = 1 - *p*².

   So, cos(θ) = ±√(1 - *p*²).

   Since θ lies in the second quadrant, cosine is negative. Therefore,

   **cos(θ) = -√(1 - *p*²)**

   *Justification:* In the second quadrant (π/2 < θ < π), the x-coordinate is negative, and cosine represents the x-coordinate on the unit circle.

2. **Tangent (tan θ):**

   We know that tan(θ) = sin(θ) / cos(θ).

   Therefore, tan(θ) = *p* / (-√(1 - *p*²)) = **- *p* / √(1 - *p*²)**

   *Justification:* In the second quadrant, sine is positive, and cosine is negative. Therefore, tangent (sine/cosine) is negative.

3. **Cosecant (csc θ):**

   Cosecant is the reciprocal of sine: csc(θ) = 1 / sin(θ).

   Therefore, csc(θ) = **1 / *p***

   *Justification:* In the second quadrant, sine is positive. Therefore, its reciprocal, cosecant, is also positive.

4. **Secant (sec θ):**

   Secant is the reciprocal of cosine: sec(θ) = 1 / cos(θ).

   Therefore, sec(θ) = 1 / (-√(1 - *p*²)) = **-1 / √(1 - *p*²)**

   *Justification:* In the second quadrant, cosine is negative. Therefore, its reciprocal, secant, is also negative.

5. **Cotangent (cot θ):**

   Cotangent is the reciprocal of tangent: cot(θ) = 1 / tan(θ).

   Therefore, cot(θ) = 1 / (- *p* / √(1 - *p*²)) = **-√(1 - *p*²) / *p***

   *Justification:* In the second quadrant, tangent is negative. Therefore, its reciprocal, cotangent, is also negative.

**Summary Table:**

| Trigonometric Ratio | Value in terms of *p* | Sign in Quadrant II |
|-----------------------|---------------------------------|---------------------|
| sin(θ)                | *p*                               | +                   |
| cos(θ)                | -√(1 - *p*²)                     | -                   |
| tan(θ)                | - *p* / √(1 - *p*²)              | -                   |
| csc(θ)                | 1 / *p*                           | +                   |
| sec(θ)                | -1 / √(1 - *p*²)                  | -                   |
| cot(θ)                | -√(1 - *p*²) / *p*              | -                   |

*   **Pythagorean Identity:** The fundamental identity sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1 is the foundation for solving many trigonometric problems.
*   **Reciprocal Identities:** Remember the definitions of cosecant, secant, and cotangent as reciprocals of sine, cosine, and tangent, respectively.

3.  Without using a calculator, prove the following trigonometric identity:
Sample Answer:
Okay, here's a sample answer suitable for a CBSE Class 11-12 student, based on the provided NCERT chapter content.  I'll assume the identity to be proven is one that's reasonably likely to appear based on the examples and exercises referenced. Let's assume the identity to be proven is:

**Prove that:  `tan 3x tan 2x tan x = tan 3x - tan 2x - tan x`**

**Answer:**

To prove the trigonometric identity `tan 3x tan 2x tan x = tan 3x - tan 2x - tan x`, we will start with the identity for `tan(A - B)`.

We know that:

`tan(A - B) = (tan A - tan B) / (1 + tan A tan B)`

Now, consider `3x = 2x + x`.  Therefore, we can write:

`tan 3x = tan(2x + x)`

Using the tangent addition formula:  `tan(A + B) = (tan A + tan B) / (1 - tan A tan B)`, we get:

`tan 3x = (tan 2x + tan x) / (1 - tan 2x tan x)`

Now, multiply both sides by `(1 - tan 2x tan x)`:

`tan 3x * (1 - tan 2x tan x) = tan 2x + tan x`

Expanding the left side:

`tan 3x - tan 3x tan 2x tan x = tan 2x + tan x`

Now, rearrange the terms to isolate the desired expression:

`tan 3x - tan 2x - tan x = tan 3x tan 2x tan x`

Therefore:

`tan 3x tan 2x tan x = tan 3x - tan 2x - tan x`

**Hence Proved.**

**Explanation of Steps:**

1.  **Start with a Known Identity:** The solution begins by recalling the tangent addition formula, a fundamental trigonometric identity.

2.  **Relate the Angles:** The problem cleverly utilizes the relationship `3x = 2x + x` to connect the terms in the desired identity.

3.  **Apply the Addition Formula:** The tangent addition formula is applied to `tan(2x + x)` to express `tan 3x` in terms of `tan 2x` and `tan x`.

4.  **Rearrange and Simplify:**  The equation is then manipulated algebraically to isolate the term `tan 3x tan 2x tan x` on one side, resulting in the required identity.  The key step is multiplying both sides by the denominator and then carefully rearranging the terms.
