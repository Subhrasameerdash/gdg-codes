**Solved Example: Determining Empirical Formula**

**Problem:** A 0.25 g sample of an organic compound containing carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen is completely burned in excess oxygen. The products are 0.51 g of carbon dioxide (CO2) and 0.21 g of water (H2O). Determine the empirical formula of the compound.

**Solution:**

1.  **Calculate the mass of carbon:**
    *   1 mole of CO2 (44 g) contains 1 mole of C (12 g).
    *   Mass of C in 0.51 g CO2 = (12 g C / 44 g CO2) * 0.51 g CO2 = 0.139 g C

2.  **Calculate the mass of hydrogen:**
    *   1 mole of H2O (18 g) contains 2 moles of H (2 g).
    *   Mass of H in 0.21 g H2O = (2 g H / 18 g H2O) * 0.21 g H2O = 0.023 g H

3.  **Calculate the mass of oxygen:**
    *   Mass of O = Total mass of compound - (Mass of C + Mass of H)
    *   Mass of O = 0.25 g - (0.139 g + 0.023 g) = 0.088 g O

4.  **Convert masses to moles:**
    *   Moles of C = 0.139 g / 12 g/mol = 0.0116 mol
    *   Moles of H = 0.023 g / 1 g/mol = 0.023 mol
    *   Moles of O = 0.088 g / 16 g/mol = 0.0055 mol

5.  **Find the simplest whole-number ratio:**
    *   Divide each mole value by the smallest mole value (0.0055):
        *   C: 0.0116 / 0.0055 ≈ 2.1
        *   H: 0.023 / 0.0055 ≈ 4.2
        *   O: 0.0055 / 0.0055 = 1
    *   Multiply by a small whole number (2) to get whole numbers for all elements.
         *   C: 4.2
         *   H: 8.4
         *   O: 2
    *   Round to the nearest whole number
         *   C: 4
         *   H: 8
         *   O: 2

6.  **Empirical Formula:** C4H8O2

**Definitions (Key Concepts):**

*   **Percentage Composition:** Finding what percent of a compound's mass is made up of a specific element. (e.g., calculating the % of Nitrogen, Chlorine or Sulfur).

*   **IUPAC Nomenclature:** A standardized system for naming organic chemical compounds. It provides a unique and unambiguous name for every organic compound, avoiding confusion caused by common names.

*   **Isomers:** Molecules that have the same molecular formula but different structural arrangements. This can lead to variations in physical and chemical properties.  *Structural isomers* differ in their connectivity (different bonding arrangements), while *geometric isomers* (cis/trans) differ in the spatial arrangement of groups around a double bond or ring.

*   **Resonance:** A phenomenon where the actual structure of a molecule is represented by a combination of two or more Lewis structures (canonical forms). This occurs when electrons are delocalized (spread out) over multiple atoms, increasing stability. Benzene is a classic example.

*   **Aromaticity:** A special property of cyclic, planar molecules with a specific number of pi electrons (following Hückel's rule: 4n+2 pi electrons). Aromatic compounds are unusually stable and undergo substitution reactions rather than addition reactions.

*   **Combustion Reactions:** Chemical reactions involving rapid reaction between a substance with an oxidant, usually oxygen, to produce heat and light. Complete combustion of hydrocarbons yields carbon dioxide and water.

**Summary:**

This selection of problems covers a range of topics in organic chemistry, including: determining empirical formulas from combustion data, nomenclature of organic compounds (alkenes, alkynes, benzene derivatives), isomerism (structural and cis/trans), reaction types (combustion, addition, elimination), resonance, aromaticity, and directing effects in aromatic substitution.

**Definitions and Key Concepts:**

*   **Combustion Analysis:** This technique is used to determine the empirical formula of a compound by burning a known mass of the substance and measuring the masses of carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O) produced. The masses of C and H in the original compound can then be calculated.
*   **Nomenclature:**  Naming organic compounds follows specific IUPAC rules. This includes identifying the parent chain, functional groups, and substituents, and then assigning appropriate locants (numbers) to indicate their positions.
*   **Isomerism:** Isomers are molecules with the same molecular formula but different structural arrangements. Structural isomers differ in the connectivity of atoms. Stereoisomers (like cis/trans) have the same connectivity but differ in the spatial arrangement of atoms. Cis isomers have substituents on the same side of a double bond, while trans isomers have them on opposite sides.
*   **Resonance:**  Resonance occurs when a molecule can be represented by two or more Lewis structures that differ only in the distribution of electrons (not the positions of atoms). The actual structure is a hybrid of these resonance forms, leading to increased stability.
*   **Aromaticity:** Aromatic compounds are cyclic, planar molecules with a system of delocalized pi electrons that follows Hückel's rule (4n+2 pi electrons). This delocalization leads to exceptional stability.
*   **Directing Effects:** Substituents on a benzene ring influence the position where further substitution will occur. Electron-donating groups (like methyl) are ortho/para-directing, while electron-withdrawing groups (like nitro) are meta-directing.

**Solved Example:**

**Problem:** An organic compound containing only carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen is subjected to combustion analysis. 0.450 g of the compound yields 0.990 g of CO2 and 0.405 g of H2O. Determine the empirical formula of the compound.

**Solution:**

1.  **Calculate the mass of carbon:**

    *   Moles of CO2 = mass / molar mass = 0.990 g / 44.01 g/mol = 0.0225 mol
    *   Moles of C = Moles of CO2 = 0.0225 mol
    *   Mass of C = moles \* atomic mass = 0.0225 mol \* 12.01 g/mol = 0.270 g

2.  **Calculate the mass of hydrogen:**

    *   Moles of H2O = mass / molar mass = 0.405 g / 18.02 g/mol = 0.0225 mol
    *   Moles of H = 2 \* Moles of H2O = 0.0450 mol
    *   Mass of H = moles \* atomic mass = 0.0450 mol \* 1.008 g/mol = 0.045 g

3.  **Calculate the mass of oxygen:**

    *   Mass of O = Mass of compound – Mass of C – Mass of H
    *   Mass of O = 0.450 g – 0.270 g – 0.045 g = 0.135 g

4.  **Convert masses to moles:**

    *   Moles of C = 0.0225 mol (already calculated)
    *   Moles of H = 0.0450 mol (already calculated)
    *   Moles of O = mass / atomic mass = 0.135 g / 16.00 g/mol = 0.00844 mol

5.  **Find the simplest whole-number ratio:**

    *   Divide each mole value by the smallest mole value (0.00844):
        *   C: 0.0225 / 0.00844 ≈ 2.67
        *   H: 0.0450 / 0.00844 ≈ 5.33
        *   O: 0.00844 / 0.00844 = 1
    *   Multiply by a factor to get whole numbers (in this case, multiply by 3):
        *   C: 2.67 \* 3 ≈ 8
        *   H: 5.33 \* 3 ≈ 16
        *   O: 1 \* 3 = 3

6.  **Write the empirical formula:**

    *   The empirical formula is C8H16O3.
