### Definitions ###
Okay, here's an explanation of the key concepts from that NCERT chapter on Ecosystems, presented in my own words and designed to be helpful for learning:

**Summary:**

An ecosystem is a community of living things (plants, animals, and microbes) interacting with each other and their non-living environment (sunlight, water, air, soil). Ecosystems come in all sizes, from a small pond to a vast forest. Within an ecosystem, there's a flow of energy and nutrients. Plants capture energy from the sun, and this energy is transferred to other organisms when they eat the plants or each other. Decomposers break down dead organisms, returning nutrients to the environment, which plants then reuse. We can study these interactions by looking at food chains, food webs, and ecological pyramids that show the flow of energy and biomass through different levels of the ecosystem.

**Definitions:**

*   **Ecosystem:** A functional unit of nature where living organisms interact with each other and their physical surroundings. Think of it as a self-contained community where everything is connected.

*   **Biotic Components:** The living parts of an ecosystem – plants, animals, fungi, bacteria, etc.

*   **Abiotic Components:** The non-living parts of an ecosystem – sunlight, water, soil, temperature, nutrients, etc.

*   **Productivity:** The rate at which plants produce new organic matter (biomass) through photosynthesis. It's how quickly an ecosystem can generate new plant material.

*   **Decomposition:** The breakdown of dead organic matter into simpler substances by decomposers like bacteria and fungi. This process releases nutrients back into the environment.

*   **Energy Flow:** The movement of energy through an ecosystem, starting with the sun and passing through producers (plants) to consumers (animals) and eventually to decomposers. Energy flow is unidirectional, with some energy lost as heat at each step.

*   **Food Chain:** A linear sequence of organisms through which nutrients and energy pass as one organism eats another. For example, grass -> grasshopper -> frog -> snake.

*   **Food Web:** A complex network of interconnected food chains in an ecosystem. It shows the multiple feeding relationships between different organisms.

*   **Trophic Level:** The position an organism occupies in a food chain or food web, based on its feeding habits. Producers are at the first trophic level, herbivores at the second, carnivores at the third, and so on.

*   **Ecological Pyramids:** Graphical representations of the number of organisms, biomass, or energy at each trophic level in an ecosystem. They usually have a pyramid shape, with the base representing producers and the top representing top consumers.

**Solved Example:**

**Problem:** In a grassland ecosystem, primary producers create 10,000 kcal/m²/year of energy. According to the 10% rule, how much energy is available to the herbivores and then to the primary carnivores?

**Solution:**

1.  **Energy to Herbivores:** According to the 10% rule, only 10% of the energy from one trophic level is transferred to the next. Therefore, the herbivores receive 10% of the energy produced by the primary producers.

    Energy to Herbivores = 10% of 10,000 kcal/m²/year
    Energy to Herbivores = 0.10 * 10,000 kcal/m²/year = 1,000 kcal/m²/year

2.  **Energy to Primary Carnivores:** Similarly, the primary carnivores receive 10% of the energy available at the herbivore level.

    Energy to Primary Carnivores = 10% of 1,000 kcal/m²/year
    Energy to Primary Carnivores = 0.10 * 1,000 kcal/m²/year = 100 kcal/m²/year

**Answer:** The herbivores have 1,000 kcal/m²/year of energy available, and the primary carnivores have 100 kcal/m²/year of energy available.
