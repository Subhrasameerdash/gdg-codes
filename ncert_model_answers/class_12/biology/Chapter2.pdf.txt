**Summary:**

Human reproduction involves a series of coordinated events, starting with the formation of sperm in males and eggs in females (gametogenesis). Sperm are then transferred to the female's body (insemination) where one may fuse with an egg (fertilization), creating a zygote. The zygote develops into a blastocyst, which implants in the uterus. Embryonic development (gestation) follows, culminating in the delivery of the baby (parturition). These processes are regulated by hormones and differ significantly between males and females.

**Definitions in Simple Terms:**

*   **Gametogenesis:** The process of creating gametes (sperm and egg cells) through cell division called meiosis. This reduces the number of chromosomes in each gamete by half.
*   **Insemination:** The act of transferring sperm into the female reproductive tract, usually through sexual intercourse.
*   **Fertilization:** The fusion of a sperm and an egg, resulting in a single cell called a zygote. This restores the full number of chromosomes.
*   **Zygote:** The single cell formed by the fusion of sperm and egg. It contains the complete genetic information needed to develop into a new individual.
*   **Blastocyst:** An early stage of embryonic development. It is a sphere of cells with an inner cell mass (which will become the embryo) and an outer layer (which will help with implantation).
*   **Implantation:** The attachment of the blastocyst to the wall of the uterus, allowing the embryo to receive nutrients and continue developing.
*   **Gestation:** The period of development inside the mother's womb, from implantation to birth.
*   **Parturition:** The process of giving birth.
*   **Spermatogenesis:** The specific process of sperm production in the testes. It involves meiosis and maturation of sperm cells.
*   **Oogenesis:** The specific process of egg production in the ovaries. It involves meiosis, but the process is different from spermatogenesis and produces only one viable egg cell.
*   **Menstrual Cycle:** A recurring cycle of hormonal and physical changes in female primates that prepares the body for pregnancy. It involves ovulation and changes in the uterine lining.
*   **Ovulation:** The release of a mature egg from the ovary, ready to be fertilized.
