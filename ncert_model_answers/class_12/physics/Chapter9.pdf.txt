**Summary:**

This chapter explores how light behaves, focusing on reflection, refraction, and dispersion. It uses the "ray model" of light to explain how light interacts with mirrors and lenses, forming images. Sign conventions are crucial for calculations. The chapter then delves into optical instruments like the human eye, microscopes, and telescopes, explaining their construction and how they work to magnify or resolve images. Total internal reflection and its applications are also discussed.

**Definitions:**

*   **Light:** Electromagnetic radiation in the 400nm-750nm range, enabling vision.
*   **Ray of Light:** A straight line path of light.
*   **Reflection:** Bouncing back of light from a surface. Angle of incidence equals angle of reflection.
*   **Refraction:** Bending of light as it passes from one medium to another due to a change in speed. Follows Snell's Law.
*   **Refractive Index:** The ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum to its speed in a medium. Determines how much light bends when entering the medium.
*   **Total Internal Reflection:** When light traveling from a denser to a rarer medium hits the interface at an angle greater than the critical angle, it's completely reflected back into the denser medium.
*   **Critical Angle:** The angle of incidence at which the angle of refraction is 90 degrees when light travels from a denser to a rarer medium.
*   **Lens:** A transparent object with curved surfaces that refracts light to form an image.
*   **Focal Length:** The distance between a lens/mirror and its focal point.
