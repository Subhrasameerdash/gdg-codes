**Definitions (Key Concepts):**

*   **Hydrogen Bonding:** A relatively weak type of intermolecular force that occurs between a hydrogen atom bonded to a highly electronegative atom (like oxygen or nitrogen) and another electronegative atom. It significantly affects the physical properties of alcohols and water.
*   **Aldol Condensation:** A reaction between two carbonyl compounds (aldehydes or ketones) in the presence of a base or acid catalyst. It results in the formation of a β-hydroxyaldehyde or β-hydroxyketone (an "aldol"), which can then undergo dehydration to form an α,β-unsaturated carbonyl compound.
*   **Cannizzaro Reaction:** A redox reaction that occurs with aldehydes lacking alpha-hydrogens when treated with a strong base. One aldehyde molecule is reduced to a primary alcohol, and another aldehyde molecule is oxidized to a carboxylic acid.
*   **Basicity of Amines:** Amines are organic derivatives of ammonia (NH3), and their basicity depends on the availability of the lone pair of electrons on the nitrogen atom. Factors like the inductive effect of alkyl groups and solvation effects influence amine basicity.

**Definitions (Key Concepts Explained Simply):**

*   **IUPAC Nomenclature:** A standardized system for naming organic chemical compounds. It ensures everyone uses the same name for a specific molecule, avoiding confusion. The IUPAC name usually reflects the structure: parent chain, substituents, and functional groups.

*   **Functional Group:** A specific atom or group of atoms within a molecule that is responsible for the characteristic chemical reactions of that molecule. Examples include -OH (alcohol), -CHO (aldehyde), -COOH (carboxylic acid), -NH2 (amine), and -O- (ether).

*   **Hydrogen Bonding:** A relatively weak type of attraction between a hydrogen atom bonded to a highly electronegative atom (like oxygen or nitrogen) and another electronegative atom in a different molecule or in a different part of the same molecule. It significantly affects the physical properties (like boiling point) of alcohols and amines.

*   **Intramolecular Hydrogen Bonding:** Hydrogen bonding that occurs within the same molecule. This often leads to changes in physical properties, like decreased boiling point due to reduced intermolecular attractions.

*   **Intermolecular Hydrogen Bonding:** Hydrogen bonding that occurs between separate molecules.

*   **Aldol Condensation:** A reaction between two aldehydes or two ketones (or one of each) in the presence of a base or acid catalyst, resulting in the formation of a β-hydroxy aldehyde or β-hydroxy ketone, followed by dehydration to give an α,β-unsaturated carbonyl compound.

*   **Cannizzaro Reaction:** A redox reaction involving two molecules of an aldehyde lacking an alpha-hydrogen, catalyzed by a strong base. One aldehyde is reduced to a primary alcohol, and the other is oxidized to a carboxylic acid.

*   **Electron-Withdrawing Group (EWG):** A substituent that pulls electron density away from the rest of the molecule. EWGs generally increase acidity and decrease basicity. The nitro group (-NO2) is a strong EWG.

*   **Electron-Releasing Group (ERG):** A substituent that donates electron density to the rest of the molecule. ERGs generally decrease acidity and increase basicity. The methoxy group (-OCH3) is an ERG.

**Solved Example (Original, Step-by-Step):**

**Problem:** Rank the following compounds in order of increasing boiling point: Ethanol (CH3CH2OH), Dimethyl Ether (CH3OCH3), and Ethane (CH3CH3). Explain your reasoning.

**Solution:**

1.  **Identify the intermolecular forces:**
    *   Ethane (CH3CH3) is an alkane and has only weak London dispersion forces (Van der Waals forces).
    *   Dimethyl ether (CH3OCH3) has dipole-dipole interactions due to the polar C-O bonds.
    *   Ethanol (CH3CH2OH) has hydrogen bonding due to the -OH group.

2.  **Understand the relative strengths:** Hydrogen bonding is the strongest intermolecular force among these three, followed by dipole-dipole interactions, and London dispersion forces are the weakest.

3.  **Apply the relationship between intermolecular forces and boiling point:** Stronger intermolecular forces require more energy (higher temperature) to overcome, resulting in higher boiling points.

4.  **Rank the compounds:**
    *   Ethane (weakest forces) will have the lowest boiling point.
    *   Dimethyl ether (intermediate forces) will have a higher boiling point than ethane.
    *   Ethanol (strongest forces) will have the highest boiling point.

**Answer:** Therefore, the compounds ranked in order of increasing boiling point are: Ethane < Dimethyl Ether < Ethanol.
